    IntacTooth - keeping your teeth and gums healthy

    The IntacTooth app will help you keep your teeth and gums healthy.

    This is accomplished by teaching you about prevention of dental caries and gum disease, and by enabling you to track your own behavior related to it.

    The app has a built-in tracking system consisting of registration buttons and indicators. The purpose of this system is to enable you to track your own adherence to the preventive regimen. Eventually you will incorporate new knowledge and habits and this app will no longer be necessary.

    The app can be used with or without the participation of your dentist. If you are using it by yourself it will show you how to maintain oral hygiene and good dietary habits, which are both very important. But only a dentist can determine your caries risk level, treat existing cavities and prescribe additional therapy.

    You should regularly visit your dentist whether you use this app or not. This may be difficult depending on your own current habits, and other factors such as accessibility of dental service in your country, costs etc. But, there is no adequate treatment or prevention for tooth-decay and gum disease without regular visits to dentist.

    Dental caries and gum disease prevention

    Both diseases have multifaceted causes and explaining everything is beyond the scope of these instructions.We do strongly recommend that you use the resources we have provided to learn more.

    Terms:

    1. Dental caries (tooth decay) – disease caused by oral bacteria. These bacteria feed on fermentable carbohydrates from our diet and produce acids as a waste product. Acids then travel into the tooth and dissolve the carbonated hydroxyapatite mineral. If this process is not halted – cavity is formed. Note that cavity is not synonym for dental caries. It is just later, irreversible phase of the disease.
    2. Gum disease (periodontal disease) - It is a disease that affects tooth-supporting structures (i.e. alveolar bone, periodontal ligament, cementum and gingiva). While there are many diseases that affect these structures by far the most common cause is plaque-induced inflammation.The disease starts with an inflammation of the gums which will become swollen and bleed when the person is brushing his/her teeth. If this gingivitis lasts long it can turn into periodontal disease which is characterized by the destruction of alveolar bone and the periodontal ligament.The disease usually develops painlessly (except for acute exacerbations at a later stage) and gum bleeding and bad breath may be the only thing the patient notices. Other symptoms include tooth sensitivity, receding gums and painful chewing. Eventually teeth become loose.

    Prevention

    Here is a list of the things you should take care of to keep your teeth and gums healthy:

    1. Eating Habits

      Consumption of food containing fermentable carbohydrates (sucrose, glucose, fructose and cooked starch) is the single most important factor related to dental caries. Note that the frequency of taking these foods is what is important - not the amount (the most dangerous, is frequent snacking and drinking sweet drinks over a long period of time). You should reduce the frequency of eating sweets as much as possible and rinse your mouth with water or chew sugar free gum after.

    2. Oral Hygiene

      Oral hygiene is important in prevention of both periodontal disease and dental caries.Brush your teeth twice a day - after breakfast and before bedtime. Use over-the-counter toothpaste containing fluoride or a therapeutic paste if prescribed by your dentist. Brushing should last at least 2 minutes. Floss once daily before brushing. Use floss or interdental brush.

      Even if you adhere to these guidelines it will not help you that much if your brushing technique is bad or if you use the wrong brush or floss.  Invest some time to learn about the best brushing and flossing technique, it will pay off many times over. Your dentist should also train you how you can maintain good oral hygiene.

      By brushing and flossing you will remove food remnants and more importantly - dental plaque. Plaque is the biofilm formed by oral microorganisms. It is the major cause of both dental caries and gingivitis/periodontal disease. If it is not removed every day, plaque matures and eventually turns into tartar which can be removed only by in-office procedures.

    3. Regular dental checkups

      This is important for several reasons :

      1. Dentist will evaluate health of your teeth and gums. Other oral tissues and head and neck structures will also be examined.
      2. Your overall caries risk level should also be evaluated. Once evaluated, it will guide dentist in how to deal with it, which therapeutics and regimen to prescribe. Good oral hygiene, and refraining from eating sweets are not enough in cases of high or extreme caries risk.
      3. These visits include professional dental cleaning and tartar removal.
      4. One part of the checkup is dedicated to examination of the periodontal structure. It includes an evaluation of plaque level, gums, depth of periodontal pockets, bone structure and risk factors. There are a variety of treatments for gum disease depending on the stage. Treatments range from nonsurgical therapies that control bacterial growth to surgery to restore supportive tissues.
      5. Smoking cessation. Tobacco helps progression of periodontal disease.

      6. There are other factors that might influence caries or periodontal disease progression, such as dysfunctional saliva flow, individual predisposition etc.

        Saliva has several functions many of which are related to the health of teeth and gums. Because of this if salivary function is compromised for any reason and cavity disease indicators are present, the patient is automatically placed in extreme cavity risk category and treated accordingly. Saliva flow is reduced in cases of radiation of head and neck, taking some anxiolytics and antidepressants, in some illnesses such as Sjögren's syndrome.

        People with diseases that weaken immune system (stress, diabetes, AIDS, leukemia) are at special risk for periodontal disease.

    Caries Management By Risk Assessment

    One of the goals of this app is to make you aware of two things :

    1. Dental caries is a preventable disease. If you strictly follow certain guidelines, depending on the situation, you may never again have new cavities.
    2. Restoring cavities with fillings does not cure tooth decay, it is just a way to deal with its consequences. New cavities will eventually occur if the cause of the disease is not identified and dealt with.

    Caries Management by Risk Assessment (CAMBRA) is evidence-based approach in preventing and treating dental caries at the earliest stages. It is a methodology consisting of two phases :

    The clinician conducts clinical examination and other required tests according to the caries risk assessment form, and after that, out of the gathered data - he calculates individual caries risk level (extreme, high, moderate or low).

    According to the determined risk level the clinician crafts individual regimen of behavioral, chemical and minimally invasive procedures aimed at managing the risk.

    IntacTooth app has built-in option to calculate caries risk level. In order for this to happen the clinician must fill in all three forms under visit to dentist/current status. Also the therapeutics used in CAMBRA are listed under the recommendations tab. If the clinician decides to prescribe any of these, he should check it on the list. This way the patient will be reminded and the usage of the particular items will be tracked.

    Please note that these therapeutics must not be used if not prescribed by a dentist. Listed dosages and regimens are for adult patients only; CAMBRA regimen for kids is not included in this app.

    Refer to resources to learn more about CAMBRA.

    Interface

    Application interface

    Home screen

    Home screen consists of following parts (from top to bottom):

    1. To do counter

      Shows the number of all activities left to be done by the end of the day. It will become green when zero. Tap to enter the to do list where individual activities are listed, with their respective counters. Both the to do list and the to do counter are reset to starting values at the start of each new day (12:00 am).

    2. Snack counter

      Counts how many times you took food containing fermentable carbohydrates (i.e. sugars) that particular day. This counter is also reset to zero at the start of each new day (12:00 am). It becomes red when you register more than 3 snacks in a single day.

    3. Caries risk level indicator

      Indicates your current caries risk level. When the results of clinical examination and other tests are entered in forms under visit to dentist/current status the app will automatically calculate caries risk level.

    4. Next dental visit

      Info about scheduled next dental visit (appointments are scheduled under visit to dentist section).

    5. Registration buttons

      These buttons will help you track all of your activities related to dental caries and gum disease prevention. They are placed on the home screen for quick and easy access. You should press a button when the corresponding activity is taking place. (Press snack button when taking sugar-containing food for example. Press oral hygiene button when brushing or flossing etc.) This way you are registering the activity and the to do counter, to do list, adherence indicators and graphs will be automatically updated.

    Snack and oral hygiene buttons are always present. If your dentist prescribe a therapeutic agent that has strict usage regimen - buttons for tracking it will also appear below.

    Profile screen

    1. Current status

      Your current dental status (caries risk level, currently active and treated caries lesions, risk and protective factors). This part will be updated after a clinical or other examination and findings entered in the corresponding fields in visit to the dentist/current status tab.

    2. Therapy list

      A summary of general preventive measures and specific therapeutic agents prescribed by your dentist (under visit to dentist/ recommendations tab).

    3. Adherence

      Statistical data about your adherence to the caries and gum disease preventive regimen (dietary and oral hygiene habits and usage of prescribed therapeutic agents). Tapping each item opens graph where you are presented with tracking data for that item from the last ten days. The refresh button clears data and starts new tracking.

      Eating and oral hygiene habits are always tracked. Specific therapeutic agents are tracked only if entered under visit to dentist/recommendations forms. Some items are supposed to be used only a few days in a month and they are tracked only during this period. Other days in month registration buttons for these items will not be present.

    4. Visit to dentist button

      Should be tapped when visiting dentist. Opens the visit to dentist screen.

    Visit to dentist screen

    This section should be filled by dentist after a clinical examination, eventual therapeutic procedures and decision which therapeutic agents should be prescribed to the patient.

    1. General data

      Name, gender and age of patient.

    2. Current status

      Findings related to dental caries ( disease indicators, risk and protective factors).

      After entering these data the app will calculate the caries risk level for the patient and show it on the home screen. All three forms must be submitted or caries risk level will not be calculated.

      Caries risk level is calculated after this formula:
      If any disease indicator is present - HIGH LEVEL.
      If any disease indicator is present AND saliva flow is compromised - EXTREME LEVEL.
      There are no disease indicators, greater number of risk factors than number of protective factors - HIGH RISK.
      There are no disease indicators, equal number of risk and protective factors - MODERATE RISK.
      There are no disease indicators, smaller number of risk factors than protective factors - LOW RISK.

    3. Recommendations

      This section has 3 parts:

      A place to enter date of next appointment. It will appear on the home screen as a reminder.

      List of therapeutics used in Caries Management By Risk Assessment protocol. Generic names and usage regimens are hard coded according to CAMBRA recommendations.

      This is the section for prescriptions other than those listed under CAMBRA section. Tap "enter new" to enter name, directions and regimen (strict daily and monthly usage or when needed).

      Entering the prescribed agents here will enable tracking - register buttons will appear on home screen. Prescriptions with "as needed" regimen are not tracked. Toothpaste usage (standard or therapeutic) is tracked together with brushing and does not have a separate register button.

    Settings screen

    The 'reset all' button, deletes all stored data.

    Goal

    Goal of the game

    Goal of the game: keep all indicators green.

    There are several indicators inside the app:

    Snack counter. Value changes when snack button is pressed. It will be reset to zero on the beginning of each new day. The indicator will remain green until snack button is pressed more than 3 times in a single day. After that it becomes red.
    You should press snack button every time you take food containing fermentable carbohydrates (glucose, sucrose, fructose and cooked starch). This way you will become aware of your eating habits and hopefully modify it if it is damaging to your teeth.

    To do counter. Shows how many oral health related activities you should perform until the end of the day ( brushing, flossing and specific activities prescribed by your dentist). Indicator becomes green when number of remaining activities is zero. Pressing to do button opens to do list where all activities are listed together with number of times they should be performed by the end of the day. The indicator is reset on the beginning of each new day on midnight.

    Caries risk level indicator. The pointer shows your current caries risk level ( ? - undefined, red - extreme or high, yellow - moderate and green-low). State of this indicator can be changed only by your dentist. Results of dental examination should be entered in forms under the visit to dentist tab/current status (all three forms must be submitted). After that the app will calculate your current caries risk level and update the indicator.
    Your ultimate goal is to achieve and maintain low caries risk.

    Adherence indicators. Located under profile tab. Here you can find statistical data of your overall adherence to the caries prevention regimen. The longer that a particular behavior is tracked the more accurate these indicators will show your habits. These indicators should be green at all times. Of course only daily to do list shows what are you supposed to do for a particular day. It would be wrong if you forgot to brush your teeth one day to try to compensate it another day by over brushing. It will not help to prevent caries, the same may be said for other tracked habits. IT IS ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT TO REMEMBER NEVER TO USE PRESCRIBED CHEMICAL THERAPEUTICS MORE TIMES DAILY THAN THEY ARE PRESCRIBED. Depending on which agent you use it can be harmful for your health.
